Computer-aided design

and cutting.

The project
  1. Use a CAD software to build a parametric design of a pressfit construction kit.
  2. Cut your design with a laser cutter.
  3. Document the process and upload your design files

Documentation

For this assignment I didn’t know what parametric design was. I started by watching the preparations videos given on the course website. I use program called Solid works in other course and wanted to use it for this assignment, however I haven’t learned quite enough so I used Fusion 360 instead because I have done some personal work on it before.

For my design I wanted to make something that I could use. After watching the preparation videos, I saw on the recommended list on YouTube this video here. I followed the instruction made there. I thought about changing it, but it was just nice the way it looked so I didn’t change it.

I though decided that it needed a little extra touch. I found a video on YouTube that you can see here. It is a video how to make text for laser cutting in Inkscape. I used the same format that is used in the video, but I put on my design my nickname, Ottoman.

For the parametric I had the thickness of the material and the length of the laptop stand. The thickness of the material was 3.7mm I needed to subtract 0.17mm because of the laser so the final design would be press fit. I measured the length of my laptop and found it out to be 365mm.

I had two problems in my finished project I didn’t define right in Inkscape how the laser would cut out my nickname so the letters would just fall out of the top component. I fixed that problem by taking a small piece of wood and glued it under the letters so they wouldn’t fall out. The other problem was that I didn’t take in to count that the length defined in the parametric was the length between the legs but not the length over all of the material in conclusion the finished project was to big for my laptop. I fixed that by taking residues from the plate I used in the laser to but between the legs so my laptop would not fall.

3D scanning and printing

The project
  1. Use a design software to make something that could not be made subtractively, and 3D print your design (Max 4 hour printing / 100g material).
  2. 3D scan something with 3D photogrammetry.
  3. Document the process and upload your design files.

Documentation on 3D printing an object

I was quite excited to be able to test out the 3D printer for this assignment. The goal was to make something that would not be possible to do with a subtractive process. I thought it would be great to make something that could be used as decoration at my home. I looked at Pinterest for inspiration and found it in a small vase.

I wanted it to be round so I started up Fusion 360 and made splines that where accepting to my design. I revolved it around a midline and finished it up with some fillets. The design was finished, the only dilemma was the thickness of my vase and ended with having it 3mm.

I downloaded Cura for Ultimaker and Pruza and followed the instructions in Arnar’s Cura video you can watch here. I used the Pruza MK3S for printing and only had one problem. It took 19 hours to print so I left it over night to print and the next day it hadn’t finished fully. The printer had stopped after printing around 80% of the vase. I fixed that by lowering the material in Cura by the millimeters that it had already been printed and glued the two parts together. It came out quite nice. The only thing left is to paint it so it will be accepted by my fiancée on a fancy shelf in our home.

The problem, I forgot to take a picture before I glued it together.


The vase, it only needs painting.

Documentation on 3D scanning an object

For this project I downloaded AutoCAD ReCap Photo. I took 70 photos of a record player and uploaded it to the program. It had to much flash in it, so it didn’t come out well. I found a YouTube video that recommended using yellow tape. I took the pictures again and it was much better.

I used the program’s tools to trim the scan and make it better looking. I then used auto detect to find flaws in the scan. Now it would be possible to save the file as STL and 3D print it if needed.

Make something big with the CNC - Group project

The project
  1. Use a CAD software to build a model you can machine with the shopbot.
  2. Test runout, alignment, speeds, feeds and toolpaths.
  3. Assemble your invention.

Documentation

We as a group are working on doing an 3D printer. Still we decided to pick this project because it we thougt it would be fun though it's not connected to our final project.

We found a nice cat house on Pinterest and saw it cost's $300 online. We thougt the plywood that was supposed to be used in this project would be just as relevant. The goal was to make a cat house that would be cheaper but based on the desing found on Pinterest.

First was to design the house in solidworks and define parameters. Length, height, depth and material thickness was defined as parameters.

Due to actuality we could not attend the lecture for learning on the ShopBot. FabLab is closed so we could not do the milling on the ShopBot. Though we followed this YouTube video and made a simulation of the milling. The only thing that couldn't be done was the deegres on the edges for the roof, it would need a little bit more researche to find out how to make that happen. Another solution would be to make it a pressfit design. But since we are not cutting it out we did'nt think it was neccessary.
